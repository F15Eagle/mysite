## Summary
In 2025, I planned and executed my Eagle Scout Service Project benefiting [Evanston Grows](https://www.evanstongrows.org/), a local urban farming nonprofit that provides fresh produce to communities living in food deserts. Specifically, I engineered and built a 110-cubic-foot composter to process organic waste at their Faith Temple farm site.

The entire process closely mirrored an engineering lifecycle, requiring multiple stages of rigorous approval, design reviews, and final presentations. Throughout the experience, I learned an enormous amount about the critical importance of robust planning—especially accounting for contingency scenarios—as well as team leadership, proactive communication, and community coordination. 

I am incredibly grateful to Scout Troop 924, my friends, and my family for generously supporting me with their time and funding throughout this journey.
